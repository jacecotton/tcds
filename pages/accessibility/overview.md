## Accessibility for whom?

Accessibility guidelines and practices, while generally beneifical to overall user experience, primarily aim to accomodate and include those with disabilities and other obstacles. These include medical disabilities, including, but not limited to:

* Visual, such as total or partial blindness, color blindness, etc.
* Audial, such as total or partial deafness.
* Cognitive, such as dyslexia, autism, etc.
* Vestibular
* Motor
Non-medical (often referred to as "situational" disabilities)
    * Poor connection
    * Limited screen size
    * Limited input controls (keyboard navigation, etc.)
    * Focus (multitasking, "on the go", etc.)
    * Location (daylight can limit color contrast perception, etc.)
* Vocabulary
  * Visually impaired vs sighted
  * Cognitive disabilities, challenges, or cognitively impaired (not cognitively disabled).
